## Purpose

This appendix provides an alphabetized, authoritative glossary of the industry and vertical terms used throughout Volume 07 - Industry Solutions. It establishes a shared vocabulary so that industry solution owners, implementation teams, and business stakeholders interpret vertical-specific terminology consistently across the 18 industries and five sections. Definitions are precise, vendor-neutral, and aligned with how each concept is applied when WORLD's industry-independent core is configured for a specific vertical.

## Scope

The glossary covers vertical and domain vocabulary spanning production and process industries, trade and distribution, services and the public sector, infrastructure and assets, and the extensibility framework. It defines industry concepts as they are used by WORLD's industry solutions. It does not restate the cross-domain business vocabulary of Volume 06 (see WORLD-VOL06-A1), nor does it define WORLD's canonical industry naming conventions (see WORLD-VOL07-A2, Industry Terminology). Terms are listed once at their most natural entry point.

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Batch Traceability | The ability to trace a produced or received batch forward to customers and backward to raw materials and suppliers. |
| Bill of Lading (BOL) | A carrier-issued document acknowledging receipt of goods for shipment and serving as a contract of carriage and title. |
| Bill of Quantities (BOQ) | An itemized construction document listing measured quantities of materials, labor, and works for tender and valuation. |
| Bin Location | A discrete, addressable storage position within a warehouse used to place and retrieve stock. |
| Bulk Milk Cooler (BMC) | A refrigerated tank at a collection point that rapidly chills and stores raw milk to preserve quality before processing. |
| Case Mix Index (CMI) | A measure of the average clinical complexity and resource intensity of the patients treated by a healthcare provider. |
| Chain of Custody | The documented, unbroken record of the handling, transfer, and control of a material or sample over time. |
| Cold Chain | The temperature-controlled supply chain that maintains perishable or sensitive goods within defined ranges end to end. |
| Cost-Plus Contract | A construction or services contract that reimburses actual costs plus an agreed fee or margin. |
| Cross-Docking | A distribution practice in which inbound goods are transferred directly to outbound transport with minimal storage. |
| Crop Cycle | The sequence of agricultural stages from land preparation and sowing through growth, harvest, and post-harvest handling. |
| Dealer Management System (DMS) | The operational system supporting an automobile dealership across sales, service, parts, and warranty. |
| Demand Forecasting | The estimation of future customer demand used to plan procurement, production, and inventory. |
| Diagnosis-Related Group (DRG) | A classification that groups clinical cases with similar resource use for reimbursement and analysis. |
| Electronic Health Record (EHR) | The longitudinal digital record of a patient's clinical history, encounters, and results. |
| Farm-to-Fork | The traceable continuum of food from primary production through processing, distribution, and consumption. |
| Fill Rate | The proportion of ordered quantity delivered from available stock without backorder. |
| Fleet Utilization | The share of available vehicle capacity or time productively used over a period. |
| Good Distribution Practice (GDP) | The quality standards governing storage and distribution of medicinal products to preserve integrity. |
| Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) | The quality-assurance standards ensuring products are consistently produced and controlled to defined requirements. |
| Guest Cycle | The hospitality lifecycle spanning reservation, arrival, stay, departure, and post-stay engagement. |
| Harvest Yield | The quantity of usable output obtained from a defined area, herd, or production run. |
| Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points (HACCP) | A preventive food-safety system that identifies hazards and controls them at critical points. |
| Landed Cost | The total cost of a product delivered to its destination, including purchase price, freight, duties, and handling. |
| Learning Management System (LMS) | The platform used to deliver, track, and administer educational courses and training. |
| Lot Genealogy | The parent-child lineage linking a finished lot to the component lots and processes that created it. |
| Milk Solids | The non-water constituents of milk, principally fat and protein, used for quality-based pricing and yield. |
| Occupancy Rate | The share of available capacity, rooms, or space that is occupied over a period. |
| Patient Journey | The end-to-end sequence of a patient's interactions with a care provider across encounters and settings. |
| Perishability | The characteristic of goods that deteriorate over time, requiring shelf-life and cold-chain controls. |
| Planogram | A visual merchandising plan specifying the placement of products on retail shelves and fixtures. |
| Point of Care | The location and moment at which clinical care is delivered to a patient. |
| Practice Management | The administration of a professional-services firm's engagements, resourcing, time, and billing. |
| Property Management System (PMS) | The core operational system for hospitality properties covering reservations, front desk, and folios. |
| Recall | The coordinated withdrawal of a defective or unsafe product batch from the market and customers. |
| Revenue per Available Room (RevPAR) | A hospitality metric combining occupancy and room rate to measure room revenue performance. |
| Route Optimization | The computation of efficient delivery or service routes against constraints such as time, capacity, and cost. |
| Serialization | The assignment of unique identifiers to individual product units to enable track-and-trace, notably in pharmaceuticals. |
| Shelf Life | The period during which a product remains fit for sale or use under stated storage conditions. |
| Spare Parts Management | The planning, stocking, and issuing of components used in service, repair, and maintenance. |
| Student Information System (SIS) | The system of record for student enrolment, academic history, and administrative data. |
| Telematics | The capture and transmission of vehicle location, usage, and diagnostic data for fleet management. |
| Tender / Bid Management | The process of preparing, submitting, and tracking competitive bids for contracts and works. |
| Unit of Production | The output quantity used as the basis for costing, depreciation, or performance measurement. |
| Vendor-Managed Inventory (VMI) | An arrangement in which the supplier monitors and replenishes stock held at the customer. |
| Warranty Claim | A request for repair or compensation under a product warranty, common in automobile and equipment sales. |
| Work-in-Progress (WIP) Valuation | The measurement of the cost of partially completed production, projects, or works at a point in time. |
| Yard Management | The control of trailer, container, and vehicle movements within a facility yard and dock schedule. |
| Yield Management | The dynamic control of price and capacity to maximize revenue from perishable inventory such as rooms or seats. |
