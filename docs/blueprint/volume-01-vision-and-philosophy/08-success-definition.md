## Purpose
Define what success means for Project WORLD at the level of identity, establishing the criteria by which its value is judged.

## Scope
Qualitative definition of success. Quantitative metrics and targets are defined in later volumes, notably Roadmap and the Analytics Engine.

## Definition
Success for WORLD is defined by the outcomes it enables for founders and their businesses, not by the size of its feature set.

## Success Criteria
WORLD is successful when:
- Founders make better decisions with its support than without it.
- Founders make decisions faster and with greater confidence.
- Execution accelerates and follow-through improves.
- Businesses improve continuously and measurably over time.
- The founder's cognitive load is reduced while control and authority are retained.
- The system's understanding of each business deepens with use.

## Interpretation
These criteria are outcome-based by design. A larger feature set, higher usage, or greater technical sophistication are not success in themselves; they matter only insofar as they advance the criteria above. Success is ultimately judged by whether WORLD makes founders and their businesses meaningfully better.
