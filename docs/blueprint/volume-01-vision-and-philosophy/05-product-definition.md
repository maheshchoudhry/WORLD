## Purpose
Define what Project WORLD is and, equally important, what it is not, so that the product's identity is unambiguous.

## Scope
The definition of the product at the level of identity. Excludes module-level functionality, which is defined in Part B of the Master Blueprint.

## What WORLD Is
WORLD is an AI Business Partner delivered as an AI-Native Business Operating System. It understands the context of a specific business, reasons over that context, supports decisions with analysis and recommendations, and helps execute and track outcomes. It integrates operational capability with intelligence so that understanding and action occur within one system rather than across disconnected tools.

The defining characteristic of WORLD is intelligence applied to the operation and improvement of a business. Operational modules exist to serve that intelligence and to provide the data and mechanisms it acts upon.

## What WORLD Is Not
- **Not merely an ERP.** WORLD will include operational modules, but its identity is intelligence and partnership, not enterprise record-keeping. An ERP records the business; WORLD helps run and improve it.
- **Not a CRM.** Managing contacts and pipelines may be supported, but relationship management is not the defining purpose of WORLD.
- **Not accounting software.** WORLD may incorporate financial capabilities, but it is not a ledger product and is not defined by bookkeeping.
- **Not a chatbot.** WORLD is a persistent, context-aware partner, not a stateless question-answering interface.
- **Not a passive analytics dashboard.** WORLD reasons and recommends; it does not merely display data.

## The Essential Distinction
Comparable software categories are defined by what they store. WORLD is defined by what it helps the founder think and do. This distinction is the basis of every design and prioritization decision in later volumes.
