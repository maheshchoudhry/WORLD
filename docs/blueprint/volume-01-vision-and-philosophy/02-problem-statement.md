## Purpose
Define precisely the problem that Project WORLD exists to solve.

## Scope
The problem space at the level of business operation and decision-making. Excludes solution design.

## The Problem
Modern businesses operate with more software and more data than ever, yet decision quality and execution speed have not improved proportionally. Recording systems are abundant; judgment support is scarce.

### Fragmentation
Critical information is spread across many disconnected tools, each optimized to record a narrow slice of activity. No single system holds an integrated understanding of the business.

### Passive Systems
Existing software stores and reports data but does not interpret it, reason about it, or recommend action. The burden of turning data into decisions falls entirely on people.

### Cognitive Load on the Founder
Founders must personally integrate information, hold context across domains, and make decisions in every area of the business. This does not scale and becomes the limiting factor on the business itself.

### Slow, Reactive Decisions
Without synthesis and foresight, decisions are made late and in response to problems rather than ahead of them. Opportunities are missed and risks are recognized too slowly.

### Lack of Continuity
Knowledge and reasoning are not retained systematically, so the business does not compound its own learning. Insight gained is repeatedly lost and re-derived.

## Consequence
Businesses underuse the information they already hold, and founders spend disproportionate effort on coordination and interpretation rather than judgment and direction. This is the gap WORLD is built to close.
