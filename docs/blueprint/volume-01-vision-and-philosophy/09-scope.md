## Purpose
Define what Volume 01 covers and what it explicitly defers to later volumes, preventing scope drift.

## Scope
This volume defines the identity of Project WORLD: its problem space, reason for existence, purpose, mission, vision, product definition, philosophy, principles, founder and long-term vision, definition of success, and key terminology. It establishes the fixed reference against which all later volumes are evaluated.

## In Scope
- The conceptual identity and intent of WORLD.
- The boundaries of what WORLD is and is not.
- The philosophy and principles that govern all subsequent work.
- The definitions of the core terms used across the blueprint.

## Out of Scope
- Business strategy, market analysis, and go-to-market plans (later Foundation and Product Management volumes).
- Product modules, ERP, and business functionality (Part B - Product).
- Architecture, database, API, infrastructure, and security design (Part C - Technology).
- Engineering, development standards, testing, DevOps, and release processes (Part D - Engineering).
- Roadmap, implementation, growth strategy, and founder operating handbooks (Part E - Product Management).

## Principle
Anything that does not define identity is deferred to its designated volume. This boundary is deliberate and is enforced to keep each volume coherent and the blueprint consistent.
