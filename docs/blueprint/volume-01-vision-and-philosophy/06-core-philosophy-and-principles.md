## Purpose
State the philosophy that underlies Project WORLD and the principles that govern all subsequent work.

## Scope
Guiding beliefs and enduring principles. These principles constrain design, prioritization, and implementation across every later volume.

## Core Philosophy
WORLD is built on the belief that the highest leverage in a business is the quality and speed of its decisions, and that intelligence should be applied continuously to raise both. Technology should augment human judgment rather than replace the founder's authority. The system should understand context deeply, reason transparently, and improve over time.

## Core Principles
1. **Intelligence first.** Intelligence is the organizing principle of the system, not an add-on to record-keeping.
2. **Augment the founder.** WORLD supports and amplifies human judgment; the founder retains authority and final decision.
3. **Single source of truth.** The repository and system hold authoritative knowledge; nothing important lives outside them.
4. **Context over features.** Value comes from understanding a specific business deeply, not from the quantity of features.
5. **Proactive, not passive.** WORLD anticipates, synthesizes, and recommends rather than only recording and reporting.
6. **Continuous improvement.** Both the system and the businesses it serves compound their learning over time.
7. **Clarity and precision.** Every output is clear, precise, and actionable; ambiguity is treated as a defect.
8. **Trust and integrity.** Reasoning is transparent, data is protected, security is never bypassed, and documentation is never skipped.

## Application
These principles are binding. Where a later decision conflicts with a principle, the principle prevails unless the Founder amends it. They provide the consistent standard by which the quality of every volume is judged.
