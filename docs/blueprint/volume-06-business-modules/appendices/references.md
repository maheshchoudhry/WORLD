## Purpose

This appendix curates the established business, operational, and standards concepts that inform the design of WORLD's business modules. It gives readers a grounded set of well-known frameworks, process models, and control principles so that the module documentation in Volume 06 can be read against widely accepted industry practice rather than proprietary invention.

## Scope

The references are grouped by topic and describe real, established concepts and standards relevant to the eight domain sections. Entries name and summarize each concept; they are provided as conceptual references rather than as external hyperlinks. Internal WORLD documents are linked in the Cross-References and References sections. Statutory and jurisdiction-specific requirements are handled within the relevant module chapters.

## Supply Chain & Procurement

| Concept | Summary |
|---|---|
| Procure-to-Pay (P2P) | The end-to-end process linking requisitioning, purchasing, receipt, and vendor payment. |
| Three-Way Match | The control comparing purchase order, goods receipt, and invoice before payment. |
| Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) | The order quantity that minimizes combined ordering and holding costs. |
| ABC Analysis | The classification of inventory items by value and consumption to prioritize control effort. |
| Incoterms | The internationally recognized trade terms defining buyer and seller responsibilities in shipment. |

## Sales & Customer

| Concept | Summary |
|---|---|
| Order-to-Cash (O2C) | The end-to-end process from customer order through delivery, invoicing, and collection. |
| Customer Relationship Management (CRM) | The discipline of managing leads, opportunities, and customer interactions across the lifecycle. |
| Sales Pipeline and Forecasting | The staged view of open opportunities weighted by probability to project revenue. |
| Recency, Frequency, Monetary (RFM) | A customer-segmentation model based on purchase recency, frequency, and value. |

## Manufacturing & Operations

| Concept | Summary |
|---|---|
| Material Requirements Planning (MRP) | The time-phased calculation converting demand and inventory into supply proposals. |
| Manufacturing Resource Planning (MRP II) | The extension of MRP integrating capacity, finance, and operations planning. |
| Bill of Materials (BOM) and Routing | The structured product definition and the sequence of operations to produce it. |
| Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE) | The composite metric of availability, performance, and quality for equipment. |
| Total Productive Maintenance (TPM) | The practice of maximizing equipment effectiveness through proactive maintenance. |
| Statistical Process Control (SPC) | The use of statistical methods to monitor and control process quality. |

## Finance & Accounting

| Concept | Summary |
|---|---|
| Double-Entry Accounting | The principle that every transaction posts equal and opposite debit and credit entries. |
| General Ledger and Chart of Accounts | The central record and coded account structure for financial reporting. |
| Record-to-Report (R2R) | The end-to-end process from transaction capture through financial reporting. |
| Accrual Basis Accounting | The recognition of revenue and expense when earned or incurred, not when cash moves. |
| Bank Reconciliation | The matching of ledger cash records against bank statements to confirm agreement. |
| Fixed-Asset Depreciation | The systematic allocation of asset cost over useful life. |

## Human Capital

| Concept | Summary |
|---|---|
| Hire-to-Retire (H2R) | The end-to-end employee lifecycle from recruitment through separation. |
| Human Resource Information System (HRIS) | The system of record for employee master data and organizational structure. |
| Gross-to-Net Payroll | The calculation of net pay from gross earnings after deductions and contributions. |
| Time and Attendance Management | The capture of working time and its use in pay and compliance. |

## Projects & Productivity

| Concept | Summary |
|---|---|
| Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) | The hierarchical decomposition of project scope into manageable deliverables. |
| Critical Path Method (CPM) | The scheduling technique identifying the longest dependent chain of tasks. |
| Kanban | The visual workflow method for managing work in progress and flow. |
| Document Version Control | The disciplined management of document revisions and approvals. |

## Platform Services & Governance

| Concept | Summary |
|---|---|
| Business Process Management (BPM) | The discipline of modeling, executing, and improving business processes. |
| Workflow Orchestration | The coordination of multi-step, multi-role processes toward completion. |
| Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) | The authorization model granting permissions to roles assigned to users. |
| Segregation of Duties (SoD) | The control distributing critical process steps across different roles. |
| Approval Matrix and Delegation of Authority | The mapping of transaction thresholds to authorized approvers. |
