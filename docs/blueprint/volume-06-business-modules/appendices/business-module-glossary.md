## Purpose

This appendix provides an alphabetized, authoritative glossary of the business, domain, and module terms used throughout Volume 06 - Business Modules. It establishes a shared vocabulary so that product owners, engineers, and business stakeholders interpret module-level terminology consistently across the 32 modules and eight domain sections. Definitions are precise, tool-agnostic, and aligned with how each concept is used within WORLD's AI-Native Business Operating System.

## Scope

The glossary covers cross-domain business vocabulary spanning supply chain, sales, manufacturing, finance, human capital, projects, platform services, and intelligence. It defines domain concepts as they are used by WORLD's business modules. It does not restate the ERP-foundation vocabulary of Volume 05 (see WORLD-VOL05-A1), nor does it define WORLD's canonical module naming conventions (see WORLD-VOL06-A2, Module Terminology). Terms are listed once at their most natural entry point.

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Aging Analysis | The classification of open receivables, payables, or inventory by the length of time outstanding, used to assess risk and liquidity. |
| Assemble-to-Order (ATO) | A production strategy in which standard components are stocked and final assembly is triggered by a confirmed customer order. |
| Attendance | The recorded presence, absence, and working time of an employee against a defined shift or schedule. |
| Available-to-Promise (ATP) | The uncommitted portion of inventory and planned supply that can be promised to new customer demand for a given date. |
| Backorder | A confirmed customer demand that cannot be fulfilled from current stock and is scheduled for later delivery. |
| Bill of Materials (BOM) | The structured list of components, sub-assemblies, and quantities required to produce a finished product. |
| Budget | An approved financial plan that allocates expected revenue and expenditure across accounts, cost centers, and periods. |
| Capacity Planning | The process of determining whether available production or service resources can meet forecast demand over a horizon. |
| Chart of Accounts (COA) | The organized, coded list of General Ledger accounts available for recording financial transactions. |
| Customer Relationship Management (CRM) | The discipline and module for capturing and managing leads, contacts, accounts, opportunities, and customer interactions. |
| Cycle Count | A periodic partial physical count of inventory used to verify stock accuracy without a full stocktake. |
| Depreciation | The systematic allocation of the cost of a fixed asset over its useful life. |
| Dispatch | The controlled release, scheduling, and hand-off of finished goods to carriers or customers for delivery. |
| Dunning | The systematic process of reminding customers to settle overdue receivables. |
| Fixed Asset | A long-lived, tangible or intangible resource controlled by the organization and used in operations rather than resold. |
| Fulfilment | The end-to-end process of picking, packing, dispatching, and delivering goods against a customer order. |
| General Ledger (GL) | The central accounting record consolidating all financial postings into accounts for statutory reporting. |
| Goods Issue | The transaction recording that materials have left inventory, reducing stock and typically triggering a financial posting. |
| Goods Receipt | The transaction recording that materials have entered inventory, increasing stock and updating valuation. |
| Key Performance Indicator (KPI) | A quantified measure used to evaluate the performance of a module, process, or business objective against a target. |
| Lead | An unqualified expression of potential interest captured in CRM before it becomes a qualified opportunity. |
| Ledger | A store of accounts in which financial postings are recorded and balanced. |
| Lot / Batch | A quantity of material produced or received together under a single identifier for traceability. |
| Maintenance | The activities that keep equipment and assets operational, spanning preventive, predictive, and corrective work. |
| Manufacturing Execution | The shop-floor control of production orders, operations, material consumption, and output confirmation. |
| Master Data | Relatively stable, shared reference data describing core entities such as customers, vendors, items, employees, and accounts. |
| Material Requirements Planning (MRP) | The calculation that converts demand and inventory into time-phased purchase and production proposals. |
| Opportunity | A qualified potential sale tracked through defined pipeline stages toward a win or loss outcome. |
| Order-to-Cash (O2C) | The end-to-end process from customer order capture through delivery, invoicing, and cash collection. |
| Payroll | The calculation and disbursement of employee compensation, including earnings, deductions, and statutory contributions. |
| Pick-Pack-Ship | The warehouse sequence of selecting, consolidating, and dispatching items to fulfil an order. |
| Pipeline | The collection of open opportunities, weighted by stage and probability, representing expected future sales. |
| Point of Sale (POS) | The module and terminal where in-person retail sales transactions are captured and settled. |
| Procure-to-Pay (P2P) | The end-to-end process from purchase requisition through ordering, receipt, invoicing, and vendor payment. |
| Production Order | An authorized instruction to manufacture a specified quantity of an item by a required date. |
| Purchase Order (PO) | A formal, authorized commitment issued to a vendor to supply specified goods or services at agreed terms. |
| Purchase Requisition (PR) | An internal request to procure goods or services, subject to approval before a purchase order is raised. |
| Quality Inspection | The examination of materials or output against defined specifications to accept, reject, or rework. |
| Reorder Point | The stock level at which a replenishment order is triggered to avoid a stockout. |
| Requisition | An internal request that initiates a procurement, hiring, or resourcing process pending approval. |
| Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) | An authorization model that grants permissions to roles, which are then assigned to users. |
| Routing | The ordered sequence of operations and work centers required to manufacture an item. |
| Safety Stock | The buffer inventory held to absorb demand and supply variability and protect service levels. |
| Sales Order | A confirmed customer request to deliver specified goods or services, initiating the order-to-cash process. |
| Service Level | The proportion of demand met on time and in full, expressed as a target percentage. |
| Shift | A defined working-time pattern to which employees are scheduled and against which attendance is measured. |
| Stock Keeping Unit (SKU) | A distinct, individually tracked item identifier used to manage inventory at the most granular level. |
| Trial Balance | A listing of all General Ledger account balances used to verify that total debits equal total credits. |
| Warehouse | A facility or managed space for receiving, storing, and dispatching materials, often with bin-level management. |
| Work Center | A resource or group of resources (people, machines) at which production operations are performed. |
| Work Order | An authorized instruction to perform production, maintenance, or service work with defined scope and resources. |
| Workflow | A defined sequence of tasks, decisions, and approvals that moves a business process toward completion. |
