## Purpose

This appendix provides a quick-reference expansion of the common business acronyms used across Volume 02 - Business Foundation, so that abbreviations are always interpreted consistently and unambiguously by every reader.

## Scope

The list covers widely recognized, discipline-neutral business acronyms relevant to strategy, operations, finance, governance, and measurement. It excludes WORLD-specific and purely technical abbreviations.

## Acronyms

| Acronym | Expansion | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| APAC | Asia-Pacific | A geographic market grouping covering the Asia and Pacific regions. |
| AR | Accounts Receivable | Money owed to a business by its customers for delivered goods or services. |
| AP | Accounts Payable | Money a business owes to its suppliers for goods or services received. |
| B2B | Business-to-Business | Commerce in which one business sells to another business. |
| B2C | Business-to-Consumer | Commerce in which a business sells directly to individual consumers. |
| BAU | Business As Usual | The normal, ongoing operation of a business outside of change initiatives. |
| CAC | Customer Acquisition Cost | The total cost of acquiring one new customer. |
| CAPEX | Capital Expenditure | Spending on long-lived assets that provide value over multiple periods. |
| COGS | Cost of Goods Sold | The direct costs of producing the goods or services a business sells. |
| CRM | Customer Relationship Management | The practice and systems for managing interactions with customers. |
| CSAT | Customer Satisfaction | A measure of how satisfied customers are with a product, service, or interaction. |
| CTA | Call to Action | A prompt directing an audience toward a specific desired action. |
| EBITDA | Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization | A measure of core operating profitability before financing and accounting effects. |
| ERP | Enterprise Resource Planning | Integrated systems managing core business processes across functions. |
| FTE | Full-Time Equivalent | A unit expressing workload or headcount as a share of a full-time role. |
| GTM | Go-To-Market | The strategy for delivering a product to customers and reaching a market. |
| KPI | Key Performance Indicator | A metric selected to track progress against a critical objective. |
| LTV | Lifetime Value | The total net value a customer generates over the whole relationship. |
| MDM | Master Data Management | The discipline of maintaining a single, trusted source of core business data. |
| MoM | Month-over-Month | A comparison of a metric between one month and the preceding month. |
| MRR | Monthly Recurring Revenue | Predictable subscription revenue normalized to a monthly figure. |
| MVP | Minimum Viable Product | The smallest product release that delivers value and enables validated learning. |
| NPS | Net Promoter Score | A measure of customer loyalty based on likelihood to recommend. |
| OKR | Objectives and Key Results | A goal-setting framework pairing objectives with measurable key results. |
| OPEX | Operating Expenditure | The ongoing costs of running day-to-day business operations. |
| P&L | Profit and Loss | A financial statement summarizing revenue, costs, and resulting profit. |
| PDCA | Plan-Do-Check-Act | An iterative cycle for continuous improvement of processes. |
| PMF | Product-Market Fit | The state in which a product satisfies strong demand in a defined market. |
| QoQ | Quarter-over-Quarter | A comparison of a metric between one quarter and the preceding quarter. |
| RACI | Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed | A model for clarifying roles and responsibilities in a task or process. |
| ROI | Return on Investment | The gain from an investment relative to its cost. |
| SaaS | Software as a Service | Software delivered and consumed over the internet on a subscription basis. |
| SLA | Service Level Agreement | A commitment defining the expected level of a delivered service. |
| SMART | Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound | Criteria for well-formed goals and objectives. |
| SME | Subject Matter Expert | A person with deep expertise in a specific domain. |
| SOP | Standard Operating Procedure | A documented, repeatable procedure for performing a routine task. |
| SOW | Statement of Work | A document defining scope, deliverables, and terms of an engagement. |
| SWOT | Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats | A framework for situational analysis of internal and external factors. |
| TAM | Total Addressable Market | The total revenue opportunity if a product captured an entire market. |
| USP | Unique Selling Proposition | The distinct benefit that sets an offering apart from alternatives. |
| VOC | Voice of the Customer | The structured capture of customer needs, expectations, and feedback. |
| WIP | Work in Progress | Work that has started but is not yet complete. |
| YoY | Year-over-Year | A comparison of a metric between one period and the same period a year earlier. |
| YTD | Year-to-Date | The cumulative value of a metric from the start of the year to the present. |
