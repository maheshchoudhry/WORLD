## Purpose

This appendix provides a structured, repeatable framework for assessing the maturity and health of a business or business function. It converts subjective judgment into a consistent, comparable score, enabling objective baselines, gap identification, and progress tracking over time.

## Scope

The framework is general-purpose and applies to any business unit or function. It defines assessment dimensions, a common scoring scale, an evaluation method, and a worked example. It is a self- or facilitator-led instrument and is complementary to the formal audit framework in Appendix A6.

## Assessment Dimensions

| # | Dimension | What It Evaluates |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Strategy and Direction | Clarity of mission, vision, and strategic choices; alignment of activity to goals. |
| 2 | Customer and Market | Understanding of customers, strength of value proposition, and market fit. |
| 3 | Process and Operations | Definition, standardization, and reliability of core processes. |
| 4 | People and Capability | Roles, skills, accountability, and capacity to execute. |
| 5 | Financial Health | Profitability, cash flow control, and financial discipline. |
| 6 | Data and Measurement | Availability, quality, and use of metrics to inform decisions. |
| 7 | Governance and Risk | Decision rights, controls, compliance, and risk management. |
| 8 | Innovation and Improvement | Capacity to learn, adapt, and improve continuously. |

## Scoring Scale

| Score | Level | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Initial | Ad hoc and inconsistent; outcomes depend on individuals. |
| 2 | Developing | Some practices exist but are informal and uneven. |
| 3 | Defined | Practices are documented, standardized, and widely followed. |
| 4 | Managed | Practices are measured, controlled, and consistently effective. |
| 5 | Optimizing | Practices are continuously improved using data and feedback. |

## Assessment Method

1. For each dimension, gather evidence and assign a score of 1 to 5 using the scale above.
2. Record the evidence and rationale supporting each score.
3. Apply a weight to each dimension reflecting its importance to current objectives (weights should sum to 100 percent).
4. Calculate the weighted score for each dimension (score x weight) and sum to a total.
5. Identify the lowest-scoring and highest-priority dimensions as focus areas.
6. Define target scores and improvement actions, then re-assess on a fixed cycle.

## Example Scored Assessment

| Dimension | Weight | Score (1-5) | Weighted Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Strategy and Direction | 15% | 4 | 0.60 | Clear vision; strategy communicated but not fully cascaded. |
| Customer and Market | 15% | 3 | 0.45 | Segments understood; value proposition needs sharpening. |
| Process and Operations | 15% | 3 | 0.45 | Core processes documented; adherence is uneven. |
| People and Capability | 10% | 3 | 0.30 | Roles defined; some capability gaps in key areas. |
| Financial Health | 15% | 4 | 0.60 | Profitable with disciplined cash management. |
| Data and Measurement | 10% | 2 | 0.20 | Metrics exist but are not consistently used in decisions. |
| Governance and Risk | 10% | 3 | 0.30 | Controls in place; risk register not actively maintained. |
| Innovation and Improvement | 10% | 2 | 0.20 | Improvement is reactive rather than systematic. |
| **Total** | **100%** | **-** | **3.10** | Overall maturity: Defined, trending toward Managed. |

_Interpretation: A weighted total of 3.10 on a 5-point scale indicates a Defined organization with clear next-step opportunities in Data and Measurement and in Innovation and Improvement._
