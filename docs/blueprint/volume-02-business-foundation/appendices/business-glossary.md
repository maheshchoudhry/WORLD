## Purpose

This appendix establishes a single, authoritative vocabulary for the general business concepts used throughout Volume 02 - Business Foundation. It exists to remove ambiguity, ensure consistent terminology across chapters, and give every reader - human or AI Business Partner - a shared reference point when interpreting the blueprint.

## Scope

The glossary covers foundational, discipline-neutral business terms referenced across Volume 02. It deliberately excludes WORLD-specific product terminology (documented elsewhere) and technical/engineering vocabulary. Definitions are concise and written from first principles for practical reference.

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Accountability | The obligation of a person or role to answer for the outcome of a task or decision, whether or not they performed the work themselves. |
| Assumption | A condition taken to be true without proof, on which a plan or decision depends and which carries risk if it proves false. |
| Benchmark | A reference standard or comparable data point used to evaluate performance relative to peers, industry norms, or prior periods. |
| Business Model | The logic by which an organization creates, delivers, and captures value. |
| Capability | An organization's demonstrated ability to perform a defined activity reliably to produce an intended outcome. |
| Cash Flow | The net movement of cash into and out of a business over a period, distinct from accounting profit. |
| Compliance | The state of adhering to applicable laws, regulations, standards, and internal policies. |
| Constraint | A limiting factor - resource, policy, or physical - that bounds what a plan or process can achieve. |
| Continuous Improvement | The ongoing, incremental enhancement of processes, products, or services through repeated cycles of learning. |
| Cost Structure | The composition and behavior of the costs a business incurs, typically split into fixed and variable components. |
| Customer | The person or organization that receives and derives value from a product or service, whether or not they pay for it. |
| Customer Segment | A distinct group of customers with shared needs, behaviors, or characteristics addressed by a common value proposition. |
| Decision | A commitment to a course of action selected from alternatives under conditions of limited information. |
| Deliverable | A tangible or intangible output produced to complete a task, phase, or project and handed to a defined recipient. |
| Dependency | A relationship in which one task, decision, or outcome cannot proceed until another is complete or available. |
| Efficiency | The ratio of useful output to the resources consumed in producing it. |
| Effectiveness | The degree to which an activity achieves its intended outcome, regardless of resource cost. |
| Escalation | The formal process of raising an issue to a higher level of authority when it cannot be resolved at the current level. |
| Governance | The system of rules, roles, and processes by which an organization directs and controls its activities and decisions. |
| Gross Margin | Revenue minus the direct cost of goods or services sold, expressed as an amount or percentage of revenue. |
| Key Result | A measurable, time-bound outcome used to track progress toward an objective. |
| Leading Indicator | A measure that predicts future performance and can be influenced before an outcome occurs. |
| Lagging Indicator | A measure that reports the result of past activity after an outcome has occurred. |
| Margin | The difference between revenue and cost, expressed in absolute terms or as a percentage. |
| Metric | A quantified measure used to track, evaluate, and compare performance over time. |
| Milestone | A significant, scheduled checkpoint marking the completion of a defined body of work. |
| Mission | The enduring statement of an organization's core purpose and the value it exists to deliver. |
| Objective | A qualitative statement of a desired outcome that provides direction for effort. |
| Operating Model | The design describing how people, process, and information combine to deliver the business model day to day. |
| Outcome | The measurable change or benefit produced by an activity, as distinct from the activity's output. |
| Output | The direct, tangible product of an activity, as distinct from the outcome it enables. |
| Overhead | Indirect costs required to run a business that are not directly attributable to a specific product or service. |
| Policy | A formal, enduring statement of intent that guides decisions and behavior within defined boundaries. |
| Process | A defined, repeatable sequence of activities that transforms inputs into a valued output. |
| Productivity | The rate at which valued output is produced per unit of input, commonly labor or time. |
| Profit | The financial surplus remaining after all costs are subtracted from revenue over a period. |
| Quality | The degree to which a product, service, or process meets defined requirements and fitness for purpose. |
| Requirement | A documented, testable condition that a solution must satisfy to be accepted. |
| Revenue | The total income earned from delivering products or services before any costs are deducted. |
| Risk | The effect of uncertainty on objectives, characterized by likelihood and impact. |
| Root Cause | The fundamental underlying reason an issue occurs, which if addressed prevents recurrence. |
| Stakeholder | Any party with an interest in, or affected by, an organization's activities and decisions. |
| Standard | An agreed, documented specification defining how something should consistently be done or measured. |
| Strategy | The coherent set of choices about where to compete and how to win that direct an organization's resources. |
| Value Chain | The connected set of activities through which an organization creates and delivers value to customers. |
| Value Proposition | The specific bundle of benefits an organization promises to deliver to a customer segment. |
| Vision | An aspirational description of the future state an organization intends to create. |
| Workflow | The orchestrated flow of tasks, information, and handoffs required to complete a process instance. |
