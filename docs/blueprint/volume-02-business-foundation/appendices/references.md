## Purpose

This appendix records the well-established business frameworks, models, and standards that inform the content of Volume 02 - Business Foundation. It gives readers a curated map of the source concepts so that ideas can be traced to their originators and studied in greater depth.

## Scope

References are limited to widely recognized, real frameworks, models, authors, and standards. They are grouped by topic. Framework, author, and standard names are cited directly; no URLs are asserted, so that citations remain accurate and verifiable independent of any changing web location.

## Strategy and Competitive Analysis

- **Porter's Value Chain** - Michael E. Porter, describing the primary and support activities through which organizations create value.
- **Porter's Five Forces** - Michael E. Porter, a framework for analyzing industry structure and competitive intensity.
- **SWOT Analysis** - a situational analysis framework examining Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats; popularized in strategic planning literature.
- **Blue Ocean Strategy** - W. Chan Kim and Renee Mauborgne, on creating uncontested market space.
- **Business Model Canvas** - Alexander Osterwalder and Yves Pigneur, a visual template for describing a business model.

## Goal Setting and Performance Management

- **Balanced Scorecard** - Robert S. Kaplan and David P. Norton, linking strategy to measures across financial, customer, internal process, and learning and growth perspectives.
- **Objectives and Key Results (OKR)** - a goal-setting framework associated with Andrew Grove and later Intel and industry practice.
- **SMART Criteria** - a widely used standard for specifying Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound goals.
- **Key Performance Indicators (KPI)** - the general management practice of selecting critical measures to track objectives.

## Process, Quality, and Continuous Improvement

- **PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) Cycle** - associated with W. Edwards Deming and Walter A. Shewhart, for iterative process improvement.
- **Total Quality Management (TQM)** - a management approach emphasizing sustained quality improvement across an organization.
- **Lean** - originating from the Toyota Production System, focused on eliminating waste and maximizing value.
- **Six Sigma** - a data-driven methodology for reducing defects and process variation.
- **DMAIC** - the Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control problem-solving cycle used within Six Sigma.

## Governance, Risk, and Controls

- **COSO Internal Control - Integrated Framework** - the Committee of Sponsoring Organizations of the Treadway Commission, for internal control and enterprise risk.
- **RACI Model** - a responsibility assignment matrix clarifying who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed.
- **ISO 31000** - the international standard providing principles and guidelines for risk management.

## Standards

- **ISO 9001** - the international standard for quality management systems.
- **ISO 27001** - the international standard for information security management systems.
- **ISO 31000** - the international standard for risk management.

## Information, Data, and Decision Making

- **DIKW Hierarchy** - the Data-Information-Knowledge-Wisdom model describing the progression from raw data to informed judgment.
- **Master Data Management (MDM)** - the discipline of establishing a single, authoritative source for core business data.
- **OODA Loop** - John Boyd, the Observe-Orient-Decide-Act cycle for decision making under uncertainty.

## Customer and Market

- **Voice of the Customer (VOC)** - the practice of systematically capturing customer needs and expectations.
- **Net Promoter Score (NPS)** - a customer loyalty measure introduced by Fred Reichheld.
- **Jobs to Be Done** - a framework, associated with Clayton Christensen, for understanding the progress customers seek.
