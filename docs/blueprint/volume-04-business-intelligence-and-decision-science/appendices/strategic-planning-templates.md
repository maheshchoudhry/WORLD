## Purpose

This appendix provides reusable strategic-planning templates that connect vision to measurable execution. They give the AI Business Partner and leadership a consistent structure for setting direction, defining goals, planning for uncertainty, and closing capability gaps.

## Scope

Covers the Strategy-on-a-Page, the OKR template, the scenario-planning matrix, and the gap-analysis template. These templates feed the reviews in Appendix A4 and the scorecards in Appendix A7.

## 1. Strategy on a Page

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Vision | The long-term aspiration (from Volume 01). |
| Mission | What we do and for whom, today. |
| Strategic pillars | 3-5 focus areas that carry the strategy. |
| Goals (12-18 months) | Outcome-level goals per pillar. |
| Key initiatives | Major programs delivering the goals. |
| Success metrics | How progress is measured (linked to KPIs). |
| Guardrails | Non-negotiables, values, and constraints. |

## 2. OKR Template

Objectives are qualitative and inspiring; Key Results are quantitative and time-bound. Recommend 3-5 Key Results per Objective.

| Objective | Key Result | Owner | Baseline | Target | Due | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `<Objective 1>` | KR1: `<measurable result>` | `<name>` | `<baseline>` | `<target>` | `<date>` | `<0-100%>` |
| | KR2: `<measurable result>` | `<name>` | `<baseline>` | `<target>` | `<date>` | `<0-100%>` |
| | KR3: `<measurable result>` | `<name>` | `<baseline>` | `<target>` | `<date>` | `<0-100%>` |

Scoring: at period end, grade each KR 0.0-1.0; 0.7 is typically a healthy stretch outcome.
