## Purpose

This appendix records the established business-intelligence and decision-science frameworks, authors, and standards that inform Volume 04. It provides attribution and a starting point for deeper reading. Sources are cited by framework, author, or standard name; no URLs are fabricated.

## Scope

References are grouped by topic aligned to the volume's structure. These are foundational, widely recognized works and standards; the volume adapts their principles to the WORLD AI-Native operating context rather than reproducing them.

## Strategy and Competitive Analysis

- Michael E. Porter - *Competitive Strategy* (Five Forces framework) and *Competitive Advantage* (Value Chain).
- Kim, W. Chan and Renee Mauborgne - *Blue Ocean Strategy*.
- Henry Mintzberg - work on strategy formation and emergent strategy.
- SWOT analysis - a widely used strategic planning technique attributed to work developed at the Stanford Research Institute.

## Performance Management and Scorecards

- Robert S. Kaplan and David P. Norton - *The Balanced Scorecard: Translating Strategy into Action* (Balanced Scorecard and Strategy Maps).
- Andrew S. Grove - *High Output Management* (foundations of OKRs).
- John Doerr - *Measure What Matters* (Objectives and Key Results).

## Decision Science and Judgment

- Daniel Kahneman - *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (System 1 / System 2, cognitive biases).
- Kahneman, Sibony, and Sunstein - *Noise: A Flaw in Human Judgment*.
- Herbert A. Simon - bounded rationality and satisficing.
- Howard Raiffa - *Decision Analysis* (decision trees, expected value).
- Annie Duke - *Thinking in Bets* (decision quality vs. outcome quality).

## Quality, Process, and Root-Cause Analysis

- W. Edwards Deming - *Out of the Crisis* (PDCA / Plan-Do-Check-Act, systems thinking).
- Kaoru Ishikawa - cause-and-effect (fishbone) diagram and quality control tools.
- Taiichi Ohno - Toyota Production System (origin of the 5 Whys technique).
- Eliyahu M. Goldratt - *The Goal* (Theory of Constraints).
- Six Sigma DMAIC methodology - Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control.

## Data, Information, and Analytics

- The DIKW hierarchy - Data, Information, Knowledge, Wisdom (commonly associated with Russell Ackoff's work on systems and information).
- Thomas H. Davenport and Jeanne G. Harris - *Competing on Analytics*.
- Ralph Kimball - dimensional modeling and data warehousing (*The Data Warehouse Toolkit*).
- Bill Inmon - corporate information factory and data warehousing foundations.

## Financial Evaluation

- Richard A. Brealey, Stewart C. Myers, and Franklin Allen - *Principles of Corporate Finance* (NPV, IRR, discounted cash flow).
- Aswath Damodaran - valuation and corporate finance texts.

## Standards and Governance

- ISO 31000 - Risk Management guidelines.
- ISO/IEC 25012 - Data Quality model.
- DAMA-DMBOK - Data Management Body of Knowledge (data governance and management practices).
- COSO - Enterprise Risk Management framework.
