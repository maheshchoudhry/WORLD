## Purpose

This chapter sets out the forward vision for business intelligence in WORLD: the trajectory from reporting the past toward anticipating and shaping the future. It gives the intelligence framework a direction of travel so that today's design anticipates tomorrow's capability.

## Scope

This chapter covers the maturity progression of enterprise intelligence, the shift from descriptive to prescriptive and anticipatory intelligence, and the principles that must hold as capability advances. It is the capstone of Volume 04, synthesizing the section and pointing beyond it.

## Why This Concept Exists

From first principles, intelligence has a natural maturity curve. It begins by describing what happened, advances to diagnosing why, then to predicting what will happen, then to prescribing what to do, and finally to anticipating and pre-empting before a human even asks. Each stage delivers more value and demands more trust. This vision exists to name that curve, to place WORLD's ambition on it, and to fix the principles that must not be sacrificed as capability grows: transparency of reasoning, human accountability for consequential choices, and knowledge that stays current. A vision without guardrails becomes hype; this chapter binds ambition to the discipline established across the volume.

## Where It Is Used

It is used to guide the roadmap of the intelligence framework, to set expectations for how the AI Business Partner will evolve, and to evaluate whether new capabilities advance maturity without violating the volume's principles.

## How WORLD Implements It

WORLD advances deliberately along the maturity curve, adding each stage only when the trust and governance to support it are in place. The direction is toward anticipatory intelligence that surfaces issues and options before they are requested.

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    A[Descriptive: what happened] --> B[Diagnostic: why]
    B --> C[Predictive: what will happen]
    C --> D[Prescriptive: what to do]
    D --> E[Anticipatory: act before asked]
```

| Stage | Question answered | Human role | Maturity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Descriptive | What happened? | Interprets | Foundational |
| Diagnostic | Why did it happen? | Investigates | Established |
| Predictive | What will happen? | Plans | Advancing |
| Prescriptive | What should we do? | Approves | Target |
| Anticipatory | What needs attention now? | Governs | Vision |

**Example:** Today, the system reports that a key account's usage fell last month and diagnoses the cause. As it matures, it predicts which accounts will churn next quarter, prescribes the specific retention action for each, and ultimately anticipates the risk the moment early signals appear, bringing leadership a framed decision before anyone thinks to ask. At every stage the reasoning stays inspectable and the consequential choice stays human.

## Relationship with the AI Business Partner

This vision is the developmental path of the AI Business Partner itself. Over time the Partner moves from answering questions to raising them, from responding to anticipating, becoming a proactive strategic partner. The principles fixed here, transparency, human accountability, and current knowledge, are the commitments that keep the Partner trustworthy as it grows more capable and more autonomous.

## Relationship with ERP

As intelligence matures, ERP systems remain the execution substrate: anticipatory and prescriptive intelligence still enacts its decisions through transactional systems of record. Conceptually, the intelligence grows more forward-looking while the ERP continues to execute and record; the more the system anticipates, the more it relies on a dependable execution layer beneath it. Specifics are defined in a later volume.

## Relationship with Business Foundation

Business Foundation must evolve alongside intelligence maturity, extending governance, authority, and standards to cover more autonomous and anticipatory capability. The Foundation is the control that keeps advancing intelligence aligned with the organization's intent, ensuring that a more powerful partner remains a governed one.
